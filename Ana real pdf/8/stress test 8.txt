🚨 3. Architecture Gaps (Where the humans got messy)
This thread exposed three areas where the system needs specialized guardrails to handle PR and legal compliance.

Gap A: The "Rogue Publication" Protocol (PR Crisis)
The WedLuxe situation is a nightmare. A PR firm submitted the photos without the photographer's knowledge, and the planner is getting yelled at by uncredited florists. If the AI responds generically ("I'm so sorry, I will let Danilo know!"), it looks weak.

The Fix: We need a strict "Dispute & Anger" trigger in the Orchestrator. If an incoming email contains words like "angry," "upset," "unauthorised," or "missing credit," the AI must instantly freeze, trigger the WhatsApp loop, and flag the dashboard in red: URGENT: VENDOR DISPUTE.

Gap B: The Legal Document Upload (NDAs & Insurance)
Mark sent an NDA via DocuSign, and later asked for a £10m Public Liability Insurance certificate. The AI cannot sign a DocuSign, nor does it inherently know where the studio's insurance PDFs are saved.

The Fix: We need a "Compliance Asset" tool. You must upload your standard W9s, Insurance Certificates, and blank contracts into a specific bucket in Supabase. The AI can be trained: If a planner asks for 'Public Liability Insurance', fetch insurance_2025.pdf and attach it. However, if they ask to sign a document (like an NDA), the AI must escalate to you.

Gap C: The "Ignored Email" Glitch
On March 27, 2025, Jessica emails saying she is following up because she hasn't heard back. Ana replies that they did reply right away, but Jessica didn't receive it, so Ana copy/pastes the old email into the new thread.

The Problem: If an email bounces or goes to a client's spam folder, the AI assumes the task is "done." It doesn't know the client didn't read it.

The Fix: This is a classic CRM issue. We need a "Stalled Communication" background worker. If the AI asks the client a direct question (e.g., "What time works for a call?") and the client doesn't reply for 5 days, the background worker should automatically draft a gentle nudge: "Hi Jessica, just bubbling this to the top of your inbox..." This prevents the client from feeling ignored if the first email hit their spam folder.
