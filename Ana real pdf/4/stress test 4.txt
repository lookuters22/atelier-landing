🚨 3. Architecture Gaps (Where the humans got messy)
This thread exposed a few critical areas where a standard AI setup would become a security risk or miss out on revenue. Here is how the architecture needs to adapt:

Gap A: Sensitive Document Handling (Passports & PII)
On April 17th, Ana casually emails the full passport numbers and dates of birth for the entire team to the planner. If the AI is trained to pull this from the knowledge_base, you are putting highly sensitive personal data (PII) at risk of being hallucinated or sent to the wrong person.

The Fix: Team passports and ID numbers should never be stored in the AI's general memory bank. The system needs a strict rule: If a planner asks for passport/ID details, escalate to the boss to manually insert them. Gap B: The Proactive Sales Pitch (The Album Upsell)
On July 2nd, Ana sends a beautifully crafted, completely proactive sales pitch for the Reflections Photobook. AI assistants are naturally reactive—they wait to be spoken to. The AI wouldn't inherently know that it should try to sell an album two months after the wedding.

The Fix: We need an automated "Post-Wedding Workflow" trigger built into your background task runner. For example, exactly 60 days after the final gallery is delivered, the system wakes up the AI and says: "Draft the Reflections Photobook sales pitch using the standard template and send it to the Approval Inbox."

Gap C: The "Visual Exception" Rule
Belen sends pictures of her dresses and notes that the shoes in the "Hora Loca" photo aren't the real shoes. The AI is blind to these attachments.

The Fix: We need an "Attachment Protocol." If an email contains image attachments (like dress fittings or album redesign proofs), the AI should draft a polite acknowledgment but flag the thread in your dashboard so the human photographer can actually look at the visuals before finalizing any plans.
