🚨 3. Architecture Gaps (Where the humans got messy)
This thread exposed a few brilliant edge cases we need to account for in the final build:

Gap A: The "Artistic Critique" Protocol
The AI is fantastic at logistical policies (e.g., "extra hours cost X"). But when a client says, "My hair looks reddish and the background is too green," the AI is blind. It cannot see the photos.

The Fix: We must add a strict rule to the knowledge_base: "If a client expresses dissatisfaction with the artistic style, coloring, or editing of their photos, you MUST immediately use escalate_to_boss. Do not attempt to explain the editing process or apologize for the colors yourself."

Gap B: The Eternal "Wire Transfer" Chase
Ana had to chase Dana for missing bank wires three separate times over a six-month period. If the AI just says "I'll keep an eye out for it," it might forget to follow up if the client goes silent.

The Fix: The AI needs a schedule_follow_up tool. If a client says "I am wiring the money today," the AI should trigger a background Inngest sleep timer that wakes up in 4 days. If the invoice isn't marked paid in the database by then, Ana autonomously drafts a polite nudge email.

Gap C: The Context Shift (From Client to Colleague)
Halfway through, the relationship changes. Dana stops talking like a bride and starts sending highly detailed B2B itineraries, emergency numbers, and PR strategies.

The Fix: The "Fluid Notepad" handles this perfectly, but the AI's tone needs to adapt. Once the Notepad registers "B2B Partner," the AI's system prompt should slightly dial down the "luxury wedding concierge" voice and dial up the "efficient industry peer" voice.
