🚨 3. Architecture Gaps (Where the humans got messy)
This thread exposed three highly realistic scenarios where our current 5-module architecture needs a few simple guardrails.

Gap A: The "Mockup Blindspot" (The Typo)
On July 10th, Ana sends the automated album pitch. 57 minutes later, she frantically emails back apologizing because the visual mockup said "Karis" instead of "Karissa."

The Problem: The AI is generating the email text, but it cannot see the PDF mockup generated by your design software to catch the typo.

The Fix: We need an "Asset Verification" rule. If an email contains a visual asset with custom text (like an album cover), the workflow must pause and drop into your Approval Inbox so you can visually verify the spelling before the AI hits send.

Gap B: The "Reply-All" Spam Trap
When Rhiann sent that mass "Thank you" email to all 20 vendors, if the AI autonomously drafted a response and hit "Reply All" with a generic "You're so welcome!", it would look incredibly robotic and annoy your industry peers.

The Fix: We add a CC Limit Threshold to the Orchestrator. If an incoming email has more than 3 unrecognized email addresses in the CC field, the AI is explicitly forbidden from auto-sending. It must draft the response and hold it in the queue for your approval.

Gap C: The "Life Crisis" Sales Freeze
When Karissa mentioned being homeless, Ana wrote a beautiful, empathetic email telling her not to worry about the album. However, a standard automated CRM would still blindly follow up 14 days later saying, "Hey Karissa! Just checking if you picked your 200 photos yet!" * The Fix: We need a "Compassion Pause" feature linked to the Fluid Notepad. If the memory worker extracts a severe life event (death, illness, housing crisis, divorce), it triggers a system-wide freeze on that wedding_id. All automated follow-ups, sales drips, and timeline reminders are infinitely suspended until you manually click "Resume" on your dashboard.
