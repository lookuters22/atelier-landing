# ONBOARDING EXPERIENCE REDESIGN SLICE 04: POLICY SURFACES

## Goal

Bring Voice, Authority, and Vault into a unified premium interaction system.

This slice is about making policy capture feel:

- precise
- elegant
- modern

without making it feel sterile.

## Included Sections

- Voice & Knowledge
- Authority
- The Vault

## Voice Direction

### Desired feel

- editorial
- expressive
- guided

### Required behavior

- tone archetypes should feel like premium selector cards
- deterministic preview should feel composed, not like a debug panel
- banned phrases should behave like a living tag surface

## Authority Direction

### Desired feel

- executive
- operational
- confident

### Required behavior

- scenario groups should scan quickly
- selection chips should feel tactile and trustworthy
- active state must be obvious
- hierarchy between categories must be stronger than today

Authority is where enterprise clarity should show most.

## Vault Direction

### Desired feel

- more serious
- more protected
- still elegant

### Required behavior

- cards should feel denser and more consequential
- standard wording prompts should feel reusable and high-value
- avoid duplicating authority decisions

The Vault should visually signal:

- "this matters more"

without collapsing into a gimmicky dark mode.

## Motion Notes

Use Framer Motion for:

- card reveal
- preview crossfade
- authority chip settle
- vault section emphasis

## Recommended Repo Focus

- voice step
- authority step
- vault step
- shared policy card / chip styling if needed

## Do Not

- do not change data structure in this slice
- do not re-open the action vocabulary
- do not duplicate decision capture between Authority and Vault

## Done Means

- policy steps feel intentionally designed
- authority feels crisp
- vault feels serious
- voice feels premium
